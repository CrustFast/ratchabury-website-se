Hana's first rupiah

My first experience earning money or Indonesian Rupiah began when I was 12 years old. At that time, I sold handmade crafts such as keychains and brooches made from flannel fabric. The designs of the brooches and keychains were customizable according to the customers' preferences. However, it wasn't a significant amount of money, just extra pocket money at the time. The weekly sales turnover for the keychains and brooches was approximately IDR 66,000. Next, my experience earning money was during my 9th grade in junior high school. I joined a creative writing community and became interested in designing attractive book covers. Eventually, I decided to learn how to design book covers or covers for written works on a reading platform. Some people became interested in my designs and decided to use my services to design covers for their written works. The price of my service ranged from IDR 50,000 to IDR 150,000, depending on the level of detail, difficulty, and concept. Another experience of earning money was when I was assigned an entrepreneurship task in 11th grade at vocational high school as part of the Practical Work and Entrepreneurship subject. I sold traditional snacks called Arem-Arem Bakar within the school community for 3 days. The sales turnover was IDR 100,000 per day (4,000 per box x 25).
